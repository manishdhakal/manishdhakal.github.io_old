Computer Engineer & Machine Learning Researcher

Hello, I’m Manish Dhakal, a Computer Engineering graduate from the Institute of Engineering, Pulchowk Campus, Tribhuvan University, where I received a full scholarship for my studies. Currently, I am working as a Research Assistant at NAAMII, specializing in segmentation and object detection tasks within the field of medical imaging.

My research projects encompass both unimodal and multimodal (ie. vision-language ) learning approaches, for the purpose of segmentation and object detection in medical imaging. I have also been involved in a project of the segmentation task of 3D mesh data. My passion lies in exploring the potential of multimodal learning techniques.

Prior to my current role, I gained valuable experience as an intern in blockchain engineering and also worked as a fullstack developer and co-founder at a Nepal-based startup, Techniti Nepal. These experiences broadened my skill set and provided insights into various areas of technology and entrepreneurship.

My expertise primarily lies in machine learning and AI, and I am dedicated to developing innovative solutions and algorithms in these fields. I take pride in being the lead author of a research paper on speech recognition for the low-resourced Nepali Language , which was published in an IEEE conference.

I’m currently looking for new ML/AI research opportunities. My desire to contribute to these sectors and investigate cutting-edge solutions is motivated by a sincere curiosity. I’m presently looking at Masters and Ph.D. programs to further my education and give back to the ML/AI community.
